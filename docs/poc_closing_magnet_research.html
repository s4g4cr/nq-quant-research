POC as a closing-hour magnet in NQ futures

Research context

Building on confirmed POC reversion — does the pull strengthen at close?

Phases 11–15 confirmed a statistically significant edge in POC mean reversion during the main session (p=0.066, bootstrap p5=0.999). Phase 20 tests a specific extension: whether the reversion force amplifies in the final hour as institutional participants close intraday positions and rebalance before the settlement.

The hypothesis is intuitive. Large intraday players who accumulated inventory away from fair value have a natural incentive to reduce exposure before close. The POC represents the highest-volume price level — the market's estimate of fair value. If enough participants share this incentive, price should drift toward the POC in the 15:00–16:00 window at a rate above the session average.

Three POC variants

POC definitions (all computed causally, no lookahead)
Asession_poc — rolling intraday POC from 09:30 to 14:59, updated bar-by-bar. Reflects the current session's fair value estimate as it evolves.
Bprev_poc — prior complete session POC, fully formed before the current session opens. Fixed at session open, no intraday update.
Cnearest_poc — whichever of A or B is closer to price at 15:00. Adapts to the most relevant reference level at entry time.

Three entry conditions

Entry filters tested across all three POC variants (9 experiments total)
1Distance + momentum: price > 1.0 ATR from POC at 14:59, and last bar is moving toward POC (1-bar momentum in direction of POC). Requires both displacement and directional confirmation.
2VWAP cross: VWAP crosses through the POC level between 14:45 and 15:00 in the direction of expected reversion. Low frequency (16.7% of sessions) but strong directional signal.
3Simple displacement: enter if price is above POC (SHORT) or below POC (LONG) at 15:00:00. No additional confirmation. Fires on nearly every session (99.9%).

Phase 20 · Diagnostic

The closing magnet exists — but weakly

The pre-P&L diagnostic confirms that a closing-hour POC pull exists in the data. 36.4% of sessions naturally close within 1 ATR of their POC before 15:59. Theoretical expectancy is positive for all three POC variants, with POC-B (prev_poc) showing the strongest signal at +0.353 expected R.

Simple displacement fire rate

The large gap between entry condition fire rates is the central structural finding of the diagnostic. Simple displacement fires on essentially every session — it has no selectivity and therefore cannot produce consistent edge. VWAP cross fires on only 16.7% of sessions but provides actual directional confirmation: VWAP crossing the POC level signals institutional participation in the reversion move.

The 36.4% win rate is identical across all three variants because the natural reversion condition (does price reach POC before 15:59?) is the same event regardless of which POC definition is used. The R/R differences arise from POC distance at entry: prev_poc tends to be farther from price at 15:00 (having been set the previous day) giving a larger theoretical R/R when the trade wins.

Diagnostic gate: PASSED — all three POC variants show positive theoretical expectancy. The closing magnet effect is real in aggregate. Proceeding to experiments with all 9 configurations.

Why the high R/R misleads

Median R/R of 8–15 sounds extraordinary. The reason: for simple displacement (Condition 3), SL = 1.0×ATR_1min ≈ 8–12 pts, while the POC might be 80–120 pts away. The R/R is large by construction of the setup. But with 36.4% win rate and wide SL, most trades hit SL within the first few minutes — the 59-minute window is much shorter than the exit horizon implied by the R/R calculation. Costs at this frequency erode theoretical expectancy rapidly in live conditions.

Structural limitation of simple displacement: Entering purely because price is above/below POC at 15:00 — with no directional bar trigger and no volume confirmation — means entering into any random position relative to fair value. The 99.9% fire rate is the tell: a signal that fires on everything is not a signal.

Walk-forward verdict · Phase 20

Edge NOT confirmed. All three statistical gates failed. The pooled OOS PF of 1.005 is essentially break-even, and the p=0.4812 indicates zero evidence against the null hypothesis (that the edge is zero). Simple displacement has no persistent edge across time windows. The closing magnet exists in aggregate but is too weak and regime-dependent to trade systematically with displacement alone.

Phase 20 · Conclusions

What this research established

Phase 20 maps the boundary between a real structural effect and a tradeable edge. The closing-hour POC pull is real; systematic exploitation via displacement alone is not possible without a regime filter or stronger entry confirmation.

Confirmed by the data

Findings that hold across the full dataset
✓POC displacement at 15:00 is common — 83.2% of sessions have price > 1.0 ATR from session POC as the closing hour begins.
✓Theoretical expectancy is positive — the closing magnet exists in aggregate (36.4% reversion rate, R/R 8–15× by construction), and all three POC variants produce positive theoretical expectancy.
✓Exp 5 (POC-B + VWAP cross) shows real signal — SR+1.205 on 67 test trades. The VWAP crossing the POC level is a meaningful event that selects for genuine reversion setups.
✓The closing-hour POC pull is real but requires confirmation — it is not exploitable with a pure displacement entry across all regime types.

Not confirmed by walk-forward

Results that did not survive OOS validation
✗Simple displacement has no persistent edge across time windows. Pooled OOS PF=1.005, p=0.4812 — statistically indistinguishable from zero edge.
✗Walk-forward failed all three gates — p=0.48, bootstrap p5=0.84, only 2/5 windows PF>1.0.
✗V1 catastrophe rules out the strategy — SR=−3.010 and −27.4% return in 2023-H1 shows the strategy fails badly in trending regimes. This is not variance; it is a structural failure mode.

Key insight: Exp 5 is the most promising signal

VWAP cross toward POC (Exp 5): PF=1.282, SR=+1.205 on 67 test trades. This combination fires only 16.7% of sessions — which is the source of its edge. By requiring VWAP to cross the POC level between 14:45 and 15:00, the entry condition filters for sessions where institutional order flow is already converging on the POC before the entry. This is confirmation, not prediction. The combination deserves revisiting with more data — N=67 is below the 80-trade WFO threshold by 13 trades.

Next steps if hypothesis continues

Potential extensions (not tested in Phase 20)
1HMM regime filter: Apply the Phase 22 HMM transition signal. Ranging state (87.3% of sessions) shows exceptional closing geometry (MFE/MAE 4.14×). Combine with VWAP cross for Exp 5-equivalent but with regime conditioning. Phase 23 candidate.
2Trending regime exclusion: V1's failure occurred in a trending regime. Adding a simple filter (e.g., 5-day trend < 1.5 ATR) would have excluded the catastrophic window. This is the most direct fix to the walk-forward failure.
3More test data for Exp 5: With 3 additional years of data, Exp 5 would cross the N=80 WFO threshold. The SR=+1.205 result is strong enough to warrant formal WFO if sample size permits.

Final verdict · Phase 20

The closing-hour POC magnet is a real structural effect but not a tradeable edge with displacement-only entries. Walk-forward failed all gates: p=0.4812, bootstrap p5=0.8392, 2/5 windows. Exp 5 (POC-B + VWAP cross, SR+1.205) is the most promising signal but N=67 is below the WFO threshold. The path forward requires either a regime filter to exclude trending windows, or combination with the Phase 22 HMM transition signal.
