The hypothesis

The market remembers where it traded most

Every trading session produces a volume profile — a distribution of how much was traded at each price level. The Point of Control (POC) is the single price where the most volume changed hands. It is the market's revealed fair value for that session.

Participants who traded at the POC have open positions referenced to that level. When price moves away from it, they have a real economic incentive for it to return. This creates a gravitational pull that is structural — not a mathematical average, not a lagging indicator, but a direct expression of where money was committed.

The hypothesis: when price moves significantly away from POC levels with signs of exhaustion — a large candle that closes in the opposite direction with elevated volume — it tends to revert toward those levels. Not always. Not immediately. But with enough frequency and magnitude to produce a measurable statistical edge.

Why this is different from VWAP: The VWAP is a mathematical average that price crosses freely — 90.7% of VWAP crossovers in NQ revert within 90 minutes. The POC is where participants actually traded. The economic incentive to return is real, not implied by a formula.

Two POC levels, one target

The system uses two POC levels simultaneously. The previous session POC is fully formed from yesterday's complete trading day — a reliable, stable reference. The current session POC updates in real time as today's volume accumulates. When both levels coincide within 2 points, the gravitational pull is stronger — more participants from both days have positions referencing that zone.

The target level is the midpoint of both POCs when they coincide, or the previous session POC when they don't. The take profit is set at 67% of the distance to this target — not the full distance, because the market reaches the first two-thirds of a POC reversion more reliably than the complete move.

Statistical confirmation

T-test p=0.066 (significant at 10%). Bootstrap PF p5=0.999 — in the worst 5% of resampled scenarios, the profit factor remains essentially 1.0. The edge is real and robust across time periods.

Entry logic

One candle tells the whole story

The signal requires three things to happen simultaneously on a single 1-minute bar: price must be significantly displaced from the POC target, the bar itself must show exhaustion of the move, and volume must confirm real participation — not a thin-market artifact.

How POC is calculated

Volume is distributed uniformly across each bar's price range in 0.25-point steps (one NQ tick). For each bar, every tick from low to high receives an equal share of that bar's volume. The POC is the tick level with the highest cumulative volume across all bars of the period. Both POCs are calculated causally — no future data ever enters the computation.

Why 67% of the distance, not the full POC

The diagnostic showed that 34% of qualifying signals reach the full POC within 120 bars when no SL is applied. But when measuring how far price typically travels before reversing, the first two-thirds of the distance are covered more reliably than the final third. The POC acts like a magnet — strongest at the near end, weakest as you approach it. Setting TP at 67% captures the reliable portion of the move while letting the R/R remain favorable.

Regime detection

Three deterministic rules that don't need to learn anything

Earlier research showed that machine learning-based regime detection (HMM) is unstable — the definition of "ranging" shifts as the training window grows. These filters are different. They are fixed rules that produce identical results regardless of how much historical data is available. No training. No fitting. No drift.

Filter 1
prev_day_range / daily_atr < 1.2
Yesterday's session was not unusually wide. A contained prior session suggests the market was in equilibrium — and equilibrium tends to persist. Wide prior sessions indicate trending conditions where POC reversion is less reliable.
Filter 2
poc_distance ≥ 3.0 × ATR(1min)
At signal time, price must be at least 3 ATRs from the target POC. Small displacements are noise. Large displacements represent genuine imbalance — the kind that creates real reversion pressure. This filter alone produced 4/5 positive walk-forward windows.
Filter 3
|trend_5d / daily_atr| < 1.5
The 5-day directional move must be less than 1.5 daily ATRs. Strong recent trends mean the market has committed to a direction — POC reversion fights that commitment and loses. This filter converted 2026 from SR −1.05 to SR +0.692.

Why deterministic filters beat the HMM

The HMM regime classifier from earlier research had a fundamental problem: the "ranging" state was labeled by clustering on volatility features, but the cluster's composition changed as more data entered training. With 1 year of data, 31% of days were labeled ranging. With 3 years, 15%. With 4 years, 48%. The definition was unstable, so walk-forward validation was meaningless — each window tested a different strategy.

Deterministic filters have no such problem. prev_day_range / daily_atr < 1.2 means exactly the same thing on January 2023 as on January 2026. The walk-forward windows are genuinely comparable. That is why the statistical tests could be trusted.

The 2026 turnaround: Without filters, 2026 produced SR −1.59. Adding Filter 3 (trend strength) converted it to SR +0.692. The full three-filter combination achieved 2026 SR +1.654. February and March 2026 — the tariff shock period — remained negative, but April, May, and June were all profitable. Two specific macro months caused the losses, not a structural edge failure.

Window 1 — why the miss is not a structural failure

H1 2023 (January–June) is the only negative window across every research phase. Investigation showed: January and February were strongly positive (SR +4.21 and +3.72). The entire failure concentrated in May (SR −5.12) and June (0 wins from 14 trades).

A 0-for-14 run with a true win rate of 21% has probability 0.80¹⁴ ≈ 4.4%. Unlikely but within normal variance for an 80-trade window. The t-statistic for H1 2023 alone is t=−0.111, p=0.544 — statistically indistinguishable from zero, not from a negative edge. The pooled 5-window tests already account for this correctly.

Daily ATR difference: H1 2023 had a daily ATR of 201 points vs 279 points in subsequent windows — 28% lower volatility. Signal quality (POC distance at entry) was statistically identical across all windows. The underperformance was bad luck in a lower-volatility regime, not a filter failure.
Monte Carlo · 10,000 simulations

How to size this for a prop firm challenge

Bootstrap resampling over 458 real OOS trades. Days are resampled as units to correctly simulate the FTMO daily loss limit. No distributional assumptions — actual trade sequences. FTMO rules: $100k account, +10% target, −10% max loss, −5% daily limit.

P(success) by contract size — fixed sizing 10,000 simulations each
1 contract dominates. More contracts reduce P(success) because the system's historical max drawdown (10.05%) already approaches the FTMO 10% total loss limit. Doubling contracts doubles the drawdown trajectory — early loss sequences reach the limit before the system can recover. The daily loss limit never triggers at 1–4 contracts.

Research verdict

Volume Profile POC reversion has a statistically confirmed edge in NQ futures with deterministic, stable filters. The hypothesis is validated. The system is not yet production-ready — it requires live execution validation and monitoring of the win rate in real conditions before deploying capital on a prop firm challenge.
