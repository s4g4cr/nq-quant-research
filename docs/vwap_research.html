Research summary

Why negative results matter

This research follows the NQ ORB study (Phases 1–7) where a confirmed OOS edge was found using an HMM regime router. The goal here was to find a higher-frequency signal — 1-2 trades per day — compatible with a 20-30 day FTMO challenge.

The VWAP was the natural candidate. It's a widely-used institutional reference level, it's calculable from real volume data, and it resets daily — making it a clean intraday benchmark. Two structurally opposite hypotheses were tested: that price reverts to VWAP when it strays too far, and that price continues in the direction of a VWAP breakout.

Both were falsified. The root cause in both cases is the same: the NQ crosses and revisits VWAP constantly throughout the session. 90.7% of all VWAP crossovers return to VWAP within 90 minutes. This makes any VWAP-based entry — whether reversion or breakout — structurally noisy.

What this research confirms about NQ: The VWAP is not a level that market participants consistently defend or break through. In NQ, price oscillates through VWAP freely. This is a real and useful finding — it rules out an entire class of strategies for this specific instrument.

What the HMM taught us

The HMM from the ORB research was reused and recalibrated across both Phase 8 and 9. An important bug was found and fixed: the original "ranging" label was capturing low-volatility trending days (OR/ATR = 26.31) rather than genuinely sideways sessions. After recalibration using intraday direction as a feature, the "ranging" state correctly identifies days with near-zero directional bias — but those days represent only ~10% of sessions, too few to build a reliable signal on.

The HMM does add measurable value: OR+HMM produced Sharpe −1.90 vs OR filter alone at −2.57 in Phase 9. The architecture is sound. The instrument and hypothesis are the problem, not the regime classifier.

Phase 8 · Rejected

VWAP Reversion

When NQ price deviates significantly from the session VWAP, enter in the direction of the VWAP targeting a return to equilibrium. The intuition: price has moved too far from fair value and will snap back.

How VWAP is calculated

Session VWAP resets daily at 09:30 NY and is calculated causally on each 1-minute bar. It represents the volume-weighted average price of all transactions since the open — the closest thing to "fair value" the market produces in real time.

VWAP formula (strictly causal)
1typical_price = (high + low + close) / 3
2vwap = cumsum(typical_price × volume) / cumsum(volume)
3Reset every session at 09:30. Each bar uses only data up to that bar.

Strategy rules

Long entry · price extended below VWAP
01Close < VWAP − 1.5 × ATR(20) — price is significantly below fair value
02Bar closes in upper 30% of its range — bullish rejection candle
03Volume > 1.2 × avg_volume(20) — real participation
04HMM state = ranging — reversion more likely on non-trending days
05Time: 10:00–14:30 NY only
TPVWAP at time of entry (fixed — does not move after entry)
SL0.5 × ATR below entry
Max90 bars (1.5 hours) · EOD exit 15:45 NY

Why it failed

The SL of 0.5×ATR was the immediate cause but not the root cause. The real problem: entries were triggered when price was already 3–10×ATR away from VWAP. A 0.5×ATR stop gives the trade essentially no room to breathe before the stop is hit.

The geometry problem: Median R/R at signal was 9.71:1 (entry-to-VWAP distance vs SL distance). This sounds great but it means the SL was absurdly tight — less than 10% of the distance the trade needed to travel. Any normal market noise in the opposite direction triggers it immediately. Win rate: 11.3%.

34% of all signals reached VWAP within 90 bars when no SL was applied. This number — documented in the pre-P&L diagnostic — became the basis for Phase 10's SL calibration test.

Verdict

VWAP reversion with 0.5×ATR SL fails due to structural geometry mismatch — SL too tight for entry distance from target. The 34% natural reversion rate prompted a deeper investigation in Phase 10.

Phase 9 · Rejected

VWAP Breakout

A structurally opposite hypothesis: instead of fading VWAP deviations, trade the direction when price crosses VWAP with momentum. On trending days, a VWAP crossover with a strong candle and elevated volume signals continuation.

HMM recalibration

Phase 9 revealed a critical bug in the Phase 8 HMM: the "ranging" state had OR/ATR = 26.31 — twice the "trending" state's 14.59. The HMM was labeling low-volatility trending days as ranging because it clustered on realized_vol alone.

Fix: OR/ATR was removed from the HMM features and replaced with a hard pre-filter (OR/ATR > 1.2 = trending day). A new feature — intraday_direction = sign(close at 10:30 − open at 09:30) — was added to capture first-hour directionality. After recalibration, the "ranging" state correctly shows near-zero directional bias.

Strategy rules

Long entry · bullish VWAP crossover
01Previous bar closed below VWAP
02Current bar closes above VWAP — confirmed crossover, not a touch
03Bar range > 0.8 × ATR(20) — real momentum
04Bar closes in upper 40% of range — buying pressure
05Volume > 1.3 × avg_volume(20)
06OR/ATR > 1.2 AND HMM = trending
07Time: 10:30–14:00 NY
SLVWAP at entry — if price crosses back, thesis is broken
TPTrailing stop: activates after 1.0×ATR profit · trails at 1.5×ATR
Max90 bars · EOD 15:45 NY · max 1 trade per session

Why it failed

The structure-based SL (VWAP itself) was only 0.52×ATR from entry on median. Combined with NQ's constant VWAP oscillation, 90.7% of all crossovers returned to VWAP within 90 bars — making the SL hit rate near-certain. The trailing stop only activated on 22–27% of trades because it required 1.0×ATR of profit first, which most trades never achieved.

The core finding of Phase 9: 90.7% of VWAP crossovers in NQ return to VWAP within 90 minutes. This means what looks like a breakout is almost always noise. The market uses VWAP as an oscillation band, not as a directional trigger.

One signal emerged: Experiment 4A (10:00–13:00 window) achieved 2025 Sharpe +2.72 on 41 trades. This is too small a sample to trust but suggests morning sessions may behave differently. Filed as a future research direction.

Verdict

VWAP crossover continuation does not exist at the frequency needed. The VWAP SL is structurally too close to entry given NQ's natural VWAP oscillation behavior. The breakout (Exp 4, trending days only) showed 58.3% WR but the VWAP-cross SL made it unprofitable.

Phase 10 · Structurally rejected

SL Calibration — R/R 5:1 to 1:1

Phase 8 showed 34% of signals reach VWAP without SL applied. The question: is there an SL wide enough that the strategy becomes profitable? Nine configurations tested, from R/R 5:1 down to 1:1 in steps of 0.5.

The methodology was clean: SL derived from TP distance per trade to maintain a specific R/R. At R/R 2:1, SL = half the distance to VWAP. At R/R 1:1, SL equals that distance. This anchors the SL to the actual trade opportunity rather than an arbitrary ATR multiple.

Per spec: backtests were not run because the pre-P&L diagnostic showed zero R/R levels with positive theoretical expectancy. Running the full backtests would have added costs and slippage on top of an already structurally negative signal — a guaranteed worse result.

Why the gap doesn't close

As SL widens, more adverse moves are absorbed before stopping out — which raises win rate. But widening SL also means more of the price path against you is included, and NQ's mean-reverting behavior within the VWAP oscillation means widening SL captures exactly the periods where price moves adversely before eventually reverting. The two effects nearly cancel out, keeping the expectancy gap stubbornly negative across all configurations.

The closest point — R/R 1:1 with WR 46.5% — is 3.5 percentage points short of the 50% needed. Before costs and slippage. In live trading with $2/side commission and 1-tick slippage, the actual deficit would be larger.

Final verdict · VWAP reversion abandoned

No SL configuration between R/R 5:1 and R/R 1:1 produces positive theoretical expectancy on NQ 1-min. The strategy is structurally negative — not a calibration problem. The VWAP reversion hypothesis is falsified.

Negative results documented with rigor are as valuable as positive ones. Here is what this research confirms about NQ and about the methodology.

Methodological improvements from this cycle

The pre-P&L diagnostic in Phase 10 worked exactly as intended. By computing theoretical expectancy before running any backtests, the spec correctly short-circuited 9 unnecessary backtests when zero configurations showed positive expectancy. This is the right way to research: let the math tell you whether to proceed before spending compute on full simulations.

What to try next if revisiting VWAP

These are not planned phases — they are hypotheses that might be worth testing in a future cycle with fresh data or a different instrument.

Different instrument

ES (S&P 500 futures) or CL (crude oil) may respect VWAP more consistently than NQ. The same hypothesis with a different underlying is a one-phase test using existing infrastructure.

VWAP Standard Deviation Bands

Instead of entering at 1.5×ATR deviation, enter only when price reaches VWAP ±2σ bands. These levels see much lower frequency but may have higher reversion probability.

Multi-day VWAP

Use a rolling VWAP over 3-5 sessions instead of intraday. Weekly VWAP levels tend to be more defended than daily ones because more participants have positions referenced to them.

VWAP + structure

Combine VWAP level with a prior day high/low or OR level. Two coincident levels may produce a more reliable signal than VWAP alone.

Research conclusion

VWAP as a standalone entry signal has no positive expectancy in NQ futures across any SL configuration tested. The methodology was rigorous — pre-P&L diagnostics, theoretical expectancy tables, HMM recalibration, and 17 total experiments across three phases. The finding is the finding: the VWAP is not a structural trading level in this instrument.
