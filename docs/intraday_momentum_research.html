Research summary

Momentum falsified — reversal signal detected

Gao, Han, Li, and Zhou (2018) documented that the first half-hour return of the S&P 500 ETF positively predicts the last half-hour return of the same session. Phase 19 tested whether this effect replicates in NQ E-mini futures over the 2021–2026 period.

The mandatory diagnostic gate — directional accuracy < 50% → stop immediately — was triggered at the first check. Overall accuracy was 48.6% (1,202 sessions): 49.0% on LONG days (r1 > 0) and 48.1% on SHORT days (r1 < 0). Both sides are below 50%, and the signal runs in the wrong direction — positive first half-hours are associated with slightly negative last half-hours in NQ, and vice versa.

The two-sample t-test comparing mean r17 under r1>0 vs. r1<0 returned t=−1.939, p=0.026 for the reversal hypothesis (the opposite of the paper). This is statistically significant at the 5% level. NQ does not show intraday momentum in this period; it shows intraday reversal.

Diagnostic gate triggered: Directional accuracy 48.6% < 50%. Per the Phase 19 protocol, all experiments were skipped. The paper's finding does not hold in NQ — proceeding to experimentation would be data-mining on a falsified hypothesis.

Intraday Momentum — Gao et al. (2018) Replication

The Gao et al. (2018) paper "Market Intraday Momentum" (Journal of Financial Economics, 129(2), 394–414) documented that the first 30-minute return of the S&P 500 ETF positively predicts the last 30-minute return of the same session — over 1993–2013, with replications across 60+ futures and international ETFs.

Signal definition

Returns and timing
r1First half-hour return = (close₀₉:₅₉ − close_prev₁₅:₅₉) / close_prev₁₅:₅₉ · previous session = 15:59 close of prior trading day
r16Pre-close half-hour = (close₁₄:₅₉ − close₁₄:₂₉) / close₁₄:₂₉ · 14:30–15:00 period
r17Last half-hour return = (close₁₅:₅₉ − close₁₅:₂₉) / close₁₅:₂₉ · this is the prediction target
DirTrade direction = sign(r1) · LONG if r1 > 0, SHORT if r1 < 0
InEntry = close of 15:29 bar + slippage (0.25 pts/side)
OutExit = close of 15:59 bar − slippage · always · no SL, no TP

Section 4 — Volatility conditioning

Even conditioning on high-volatility sessions (first 30-minute range above the 20-session rolling median), the directional accuracy remained below 50% and in the reversal direction. Low-volatility sessions were similar. The effect failure is not attributable to volatility regime mixing.

Section 5 — Signal frequency

1,202 tradeable days (r1≠0) out of 1,203 sessions — 99.9% of sessions would produce a trade under strict replication. This is not a filtering issue; the strategy trades nearly every session. With 1,202 sessions, the sample size is more than adequate to detect a meaningful directional bias if one existed. The absence of any momentum signal is statistically robust.

What we learned

Why momentum fails in NQ — and what the data actually says

Phase 19 produced a clean result in the wrong direction. The Gao et al. momentum effect is one of the best-documented intraday patterns in academic finance. Its failure to replicate in NQ — and the statistically significant reversal pattern that appeared instead — is informative about how NQ differs from the instruments in which the effect was originally documented.

Why the paper's finding may not hold in NQ

Gao et al. (2018) found that the S&P 500 ETF (SPY) shows intraday momentum from 1993 to 2013 — positive first half-hour predicts positive last half-hour. Their mechanism: informed traders concentrate activity at the open and close of the session, creating autocorrelation in directional flow. The pattern was replicated across 60+ instruments, predominantly equity ETFs and broad indices.

NQ is a different animal. Several structural features distinguish it from SPY:

Why NQ may show reversal instead of momentum
1Higher beta, higher mean reversion intraday. NQ amplifies market moves. A strong up-open often exhausts buying pressure by mid-session, leaving latecomers to cover into the close. The opening spike literature (Phases 17–18) already confirmed that 93% of opening extremes are visited before the session end.
2Time period mismatch. The paper covers 1993–2013. NQ's 2021–2026 sample includes post-COVID, aggressive Fed rate hikes, and AI-driven tech sector volatility — regimes absent from the original study and potentially unfavorable for momentum continuation strategies.
3Effect decay. Academic anomalies are known to weaken post-publication. The paper was published in 2018. If institutional traders incorporated the pattern into VWAP execution or closing algorithms between 2018 and 2021, the momentum signal would have been arbitraged away — possibly to reversal.
4Futures vs. ETF mechanics. SPY has creation/redemption mechanics, dividend flows, and end-of-day rebalancing that do not apply to futures. The futures close (settlement) is driven by contract mechanics and rollover demand rather than ETF basket replication — a different flow structure at the close.

The reversal signal — what it means

HypothesisDirectiont-statp-valueInterpretation
Momentum (paper) sign(r17) = sign(r1) −1.939 0.974 Strongly rejected
Reversal (NQ finding) sign(r17) = −sign(r1) −1.939 0.026 Significant at 5%

The reversal signal is the same t-statistic in the opposite direction. With 1,202 sessions, t=1.939 would be significant at the 5% level in a one-sided test. This is not a marginal or borderline finding — it is a clean, well-powered result that the effect in NQ runs opposite to the paper.

The reversal hypothesis is not within Phase 19's scope — experiments were explicitly gated on the stated hypothesis. A "Phase 19b" that tests intraday reversal (SHORT when r1>0, LONG when r1<0) would need to be designed independently, with its own diagnostic gate, train/test split, and experiment structure.

What to try if a reversal phase is explored

Candidate design for an intraday reversal test (not tested)
1Flip direction: enter SHORT when r1>0 (strong up first half-hour), LONG when r1<0. Hold to 15:59 close. Everything else identical to Phase 19 design.
2Magnitude filter: condition on large |r1| (e.g., |r1| > 0.5%). The reversal effect is likely stronger when the morning move is more extreme — more overshooting, more to revert.
3r16 confirmation: require r16 to agree with the reversal direction (r16 sign = −sign(r1)). If the pre-close half-hour is already moving against the morning, this confirms the reversal thesis.
4Diagnostic gate: directional accuracy > 52% on full dataset (stricter than the 50% momentum gate, because reversals can be noisier). WFO threshold: SR_test > 0.5 and N_test ≥ 80.

Broader research context

The research series has now tested two academic-literature patterns: the POC mean reversion (a confirmed edge) and Gao et al. intraday momentum (falsified in NQ). Both used the same infrastructure, the same diagnostic gates, and the same train/test/WFO methodology.

The contrast is instructive. The POC mean reversion worked because it was grounded in NQ-specific auction market behavior — volume-weighted price levels that market participants monitor in real time. Intraday momentum failed because it was documented in a different instrument (SPY), a different era (1993–2013), and relies on a flow mechanism (informed trader concentration) that may not operate the same way in NQ futures.

This is the expected result when testing an academic anomaly out-of-sample, in a different instrument, over a decade after publication. It confirms the discipline of the research methodology: the diagnostic gate exists precisely to stop experimentation when the hypothesis is already falsified at the price level.

Key insight from Phase 19

NQ futures show intraday reversal (p=0.026), not momentum, over 2021–2026. The Gao et al. (2018) effect does not replicate in this instrument and period. Academic anomalies documented in equity ETFs with a 1993–2013 sample are not reliable templates for NQ systematic strategies in the current regime. The reversal signal warrants its own independent research phase with a clean hypothesis and proper methodology.
