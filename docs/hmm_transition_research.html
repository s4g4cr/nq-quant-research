Using HMM transition matrices as predictive signals

Prior HMM usage in this research used the model as a classifier — state today → trade today. Phase 22 introduces the correct predictive use: the transition matrix T[i][j] = P(state_j tomorrow | state_i today). If yesterday was bearish, the transition matrix tells us tomorrow's regime probability before the session opens.

Phase 22 · Conceptual framework

Descriptive vs predictive HMM — the correct use of transition matrices

All prior HMM usage in this research (Phases 1–7, 12–13) was descriptive: classify today's state based on today's features, then act on that classification. This is the classifier use of HMM. Phase 22 introduces the predictive use.

A Hidden Markov Model with N states produces a transition matrix T of shape (N×N) where T[i][j] = P(state_j tomorrow | state_i today). This is derived from the Markov property of regime sequences: the future depends only on the present state, not the full history. The transition matrix is estimated on training data and then applied forward to test data — it is a genuinely causal, forward-looking signal.

Hamilton (1989) — Econometrica: "A New Approach to the Economic Analysis of Nonstationary Time Series and the Business Cycle." One of the most cited papers in financial econometrics, establishing the regime-switching framework. The Markov transition property is the foundation: if we know the current regime, we have an optimal predictor of tomorrow's regime without additional information.

Phase 22 · Transition matrix

NQ regimes are highly persistent — and the signal is binary

The 3-state transition matrix reveals strong diagonal persistence: bearish states stay bearish 74.3% of the time, ranging states stay ranging 87.3%, and bullish states stay bullish 67.9%. Directional accuracy 82.6% vs 33.3% baseline (p≈0.000).

The matrix has strong diagonals and almost no bullish↔bearish direct transitions (0.8% and 3.8%). Regimes change via the ranging state as an intermediate buffer. This is economically meaningful: the market rarely jumps directly from a risk-on to a risk-off day; there is typically a transitional period of compressed, directionless price action before a new regime establishes.

Key structural finding: the threshold parameter is irrelevant

Phase 22C discovered: The P(bearish) threshold used as a signal gate is entirely irrelevant. Relaxing the threshold from 0.60 to 0.40 produced zero new signal days (identical N=22 test trades for thresholds 0.40 through 0.70). Setting threshold > 0.743 (the diagonal) produced zero trades. The reason: the transition matrix has a bimodal structure. P(bearish tomorrow) is either 74.3% (if yesterday = bearish) or <10–11% (if yesterday = ranging or bullish). There is no gradient between 0.40 and 0.74 to exploit. The signal reduces to a binary: did yesterday's Viterbi state = bearish?

Phase 22B–22C · Strategy

Fading the opening spike on bearish regime days

On days following a bearish Viterbi state, 62% of sessions open with a spike that reverses and drops 311 pts by 14:37. Phase 22B designs a systematic entry to capture this pattern with ATR-adaptive stop loss and prev_poc as target.

Entry geometry — derived from spike volatility analysis

Session-level parameter construction (all causal)
1prev_atr = 20-day rolling mean of prior session ranges (shift(1) applied — no same-day data)
2expected_spike = 0.662 × prev_atr (derived from spike/ATR regression: r=+0.259, p=0.0015, 90% coverage)
3entry_trigger = open_930 + (1/3) × expected_spike = open + 0.221 × prev_atr
4sl_price = open_930 + expected_spike + 5 pts (0.662×ATR + buffer)
5avg_vol_20 = 20-day rolling mean of prior session volumes (causal)

Complete entry rules

Five mandatory conditions (all must be true)
SSignal active: state_{t-1} == bearish AND state_{t-2} == bearish (consecutive filter, Phase 22C)
1Price reached trigger: bar_high ≥ entry_trigger (= open + 0.221×ATR)
2Bearish rejection bar: bar_close < bar_open
3Closed below trigger: bar_close < entry_trigger (did not accept higher price)
4Volume confirmation: bar_volume > 1.2 × avg_bar_vol (elevated participation)
WEntry window: 09:30–10:00 only (Phase 22C finding: tighter window improves PF 1.65→2.20)
Dprev_poc gate: session skipped if prev_poc ≥ entry_trigger (TP-D prerequisite)

Why 09:30–10:00 only

Phase 22C window scan tested entry windows from 10:00 to 12:00. The counterintuitive result: the narrowest window produces the highest quality trades.

The N≥60 problem — a structural constraint

Why WFO cannot run: The TP-D constraint (prev_poc < entry_trigger) is the binding limiter. Of 89 signal days in the 18-month test period, only 15–27 satisfy all five entry conditions including the prev_poc gate. The strategy generates approximately 1.3 trades/month. To reach N=60 for WFO would require ~46 months of test data. Parameter relaxation (threshold, entry window) cannot solve this — the prev_poc filter is the constraint, and relaxing it changes the strategy's edge profile.

Phase 22C — threshold scan (Part A)

Five HMM thresholds tested (0.40, 0.50, 0.60, 0.70, 0.743). Key finding: thresholds 0.40–0.70 are identical — zero new signal days added by relaxation. This confirmed the bimodal signal structure: P(bearish) is either 74.3% (following bearish state) or <11% (following ranging/bullish). There is nothing between 0.40 and 0.74 in this transition matrix.

Phase 22 · Conclusions

What this research established

Phase 22 introduced a methodologically important advance: the predictive use of the HMM transition matrix. The signal is statistically confirmed (p≈0.000, 82.6% accuracy). The resulting trading strategy shows real quality (PF=2.529) but is structurally too sparse for formal WFO validation.

Confirmed by the data

Findings that stand
✓NQ regime transitions are highly predictable (p≈0.000, 82.6% directional accuracy vs 33.3% baseline). The transition matrix is a valid forward-looking signal.
✓Bearish spike→drop pattern confirmed — 62% of bearish days, median 311-point drop, SL formula r=0.259 (p=0.0015), stable across quintiles and years.
✓Consecutive bearish filter improves quality — PF 2.20→2.53, 2026 turns from negative to positive, selection bias is reduced.
✓Ranging state geometry exceptional — MFE/MAE 4.141×, 87.3% self-transition probability. This confirms why POC reversion (Phase 15 B1) works: it operates on the dominant, most predictable, most geometrically favorable regime.
✓Signal is binary, not gradient — threshold parameter irrelevant; the transition matrix has bimodal P(bearish) (74.3% or <11%). No tuning possible.

Not confirmed — pending more data

Results that require more test data
?Walk-forward not possible — N=15 test trades over 18 months is structurally below the N=60 threshold. Requires ~46 months of additional test data.
?prev_poc TP is the binding constraint — only when prev_poc < entry_trigger does the session qualify. This filter is load-bearing for edge quality (PF 1.648 without vs 2.529 with consecutive filter) but limits frequency.
