The hypothesis

NQ participants profit-take at the close — systematically against the morning move

Gao, Han, Li, and Zhou (2018) documented that in the S&P 500 ETF, the first half-hour return positively predicts the last half-hour return (momentum). Phase 19 tested this in NQ futures and found the opposite — a statistically significant reversal signal.

The Phase 19 diagnostic ran on 1,203 sessions (2021–2026). When r1 > 0 (strong up opening), the mean last half-hour return was −0.0117%. When r1 < 0, the mean was +0.0227%. The two-sample t-test returned t=−1.939, p=0.026 for the reversal direction — statistically significant at the 5% level. This is the foundation for Phases 19B and 19C.

Economic rationale

NQ is a momentum-heavy instrument with a different participant structure from SPY. Several mechanisms may generate systematic reversal at the close:

Why NQ may close against the morning direction
1Profit-taking by intraday traders. Participants who bought the morning move liquidate before the close. The closing half-hour has high volume and concentrated institutional order flow — ideal for exiting large positions accumulated during the session.
2Intraday overshooting. NQ amplifies broad-market moves. A strong up-open tends to carry further than fundamentals justify, creating mean-reversion pressure by 15:30. The opening spike literature (Phases 17–18) already confirmed that 93% of morning extremes are visited within the session.
3Decay of Gao et al. (2018) effect. The paper was published in 2018 and covered 1993–2013. Institutional awareness of the pattern between 2018 and 2021 may have arbitraged the momentum effect — and in NQ, where the signal was already weak, arbitrage may have flipped it to reversal.
4r16 confirmation. The penultimate half-hour (14:30–15:00) continuing in the same direction as the morning is a signal that the move has been sustained long enough to attract closing profit-takers. Days where r16 agrees with r1 show stronger reversal in r17.

Key structural finding: r16 is the dominant filter

The r16 agreement filter alone (Exp 6) barely moves SR: −0.017 vs −0.250 baseline. Combined with |r1|>0.3% (Exp 12), SR jumps to 1.423. This is not additive — it's multiplicative. r16 agreement selects precisely the days where the morning direction has been sustained long enough to generate profit-taking at the close. Neither filter works well alone; together they isolate a specific regime.

The N vs. SR trade-off: Experiments 14 and 16 show higher SR (2.131 and 2.334) but N=70 and N=64 — below the 80-trade WFO gate. Adding high_vol to Exp 12 (r16 agree + |r1|>0.3%) raises SR but drops N by ~45 trades. The high-volatility days where the reversal is strongest are simply fewer in number than needed for robust walk-forward testing.

Phase 19B baseline results

Phase 19B Exp 1 (baseline, no filters): Train SR=0.345, Test SR=−0.250. The unfiltered reversal signal is profitable in sample but loses money out of sample — 2026 WR drops to 43.8%, dragging the overall test SR negative. Filters are required to isolate the regimes where the signal holds.

Filter development conclusion

Exp 12 (|r1|>0.3% + r16 agreement) is the unique configuration that satisfies N≥80 with SR>1.0. It is the only candidate for walk-forward validation. The high-SR configurations (Exps 14, 16) are statistically under-powered. This selection is data-driven, not arbitrary.

The regime problem

V1 (H1 2023, SR=−1.807) and V4 (H2 2024, SR=−2.808) are not just weak — they show full sign reversals. The strategy's short positions (r1>0) made money when the market was trending up through the close, and the long positions (r1<0) lost when the market continued falling. These are exactly the periods where the reversal signal inverted to momentum.

The two failing windows share a pattern: both are periods of relatively sustained intraday trend-following. H1 2023 was a strong recovery rally from the 2022 bear market where morning gains extended through the close. H2 2024 included significant macro-driven directional sessions where morning selling continued into the close. In both cases, the r16 agreement filter selected precisely the days with the strongest directional continuation — amplifying rather than dampening the adverse regime exposure.

The paradox of r16 agreement: The filter that most improves the signal in three periods (V2, V3, V5) is the same filter that selects the worst trades in the two failing periods. Sign(r1)==sign(r16) is a proxy for "the morning direction has been sustained." In reversal regimes this means "good setup for profit-taking reversal." In momentum regimes it means "strong trend, likely to continue." The filter has no way to distinguish these cases.
Walk-forward verdict

Edge NOT confirmed. Pooled OOS: N=271 · WR=52.0% · PF=1.238 · SR=1.188. Three thresholds checked: p=0.110 (fails by 0.010), bootstrap p5=0.942 (fails by 0.008), windows SR>0: 3/5 (passes). Both statistical tests miss by narrow margins. The signal is not random — but it is not stable enough across regimes for production use.

Conclusions

What this research established

Phases 19 through 19C produced the most nuanced result in the research series: a real, statistically significant signal that consistently fails to cross production thresholds because of regime instability, not because the signal is absent.

What is confirmed

Confirmed facts from Phases 19–19C:
· NQ shows intraday reversal (not momentum) in aggregate 2021–2026 · p=0.026
· r16 agreement is a meaningful secondary filter — increases SR from 0.317 to 1.423
· The reversal is real in 3 of 5 WFO windows (H2 2023, H1 2024, 2025–2026)
· Pooled OOS PF=1.238 on 271 trades — not consistent with randomness
· Bootstrap mean PF=1.255 — the expected PF is positive

What is not confirmed

Reasons the edge is not production-viable:
· H1 2023 and H2 2024 show full sign reversals (SR −1.8 and −2.8)
· T-test p=0.110 > 0.10 threshold — formally not significant
· Bootstrap p5=0.942 < 0.95 threshold — confidence interval is wide
· No ex-ante regime filter to distinguish reversal periods from momentum periods
· r16 agreement amplifies both signal and regime failure indiscriminately

The regime question — what would need to be true for production use

The reversal hypothesis could be production-viable if a reliable ex-ante regime filter could be found — something observable before 15:30 that distinguishes "reversal day" from "momentum continuation day." Candidates not yet tested:

Potential regime discriminators (not tested)
1VIX level. High VIX may correlate with momentum continuation (fear-driven selling continues); low VIX with mean reversion. The failing windows (H1 2023, H2 2024) had different VIX regimes. Not available in the current data pipeline.
2Overnight gap direction vs. r1 agreement. If the overnight gap and the first half-hour are in the same direction, the trend may be stronger and more likely to continue. The reversal may be safer when r1 moves against the overnight gap.
3Mid-session consolidation. If price spends 12:00–14:00 in a narrow range (returning to near the open), the reversal signal may be cleaner than on days where the morning trend continued all day. A simple r2–r15 range filter could proxy this.
4Weekly trend alignment. If the 5-day return is in the same direction as r1, momentum continuation is more likely. Filtering out days where r1 aligns with the weekly trend may concentrate the reversal into better regimes.

Position in the research series

This is the sixth hypothesis tested in the series. Five of the six have been falsified or not confirmed. The one confirmed edge — POC Reversion B1 (Phase 15, p=0.066, bootstrap p5=0.999, 4/5 WFO windows) — remains the benchmark. The intraday reversal is the closest any subsequent hypothesis has come to confirmation: real signal, real PF in pooled testing, and narrow statistical misses. It is the most informative "not confirmed" in the series.

Final verdict · Phases 19–19C

The NQ intraday reversal signal is real (p=0.026 raw), survives the best filter combination (SR=1.423, PF=1.272 on 120 OOS trades), and produces positive pooled WFO results (PF=1.238, 271 trades). It fails production confirmation because V1 and V4 show full sign reversals with no available ex-ante discriminator. The hypothesis is not false — the effect exists. It is not yet tradeable — the regime dependency is not predictable with the available signals. Future research should focus on regime detection rather than signal refinement.
