Research summary

From a falsified hypothesis to confirmed OOS edge

This research started with a straightforward question: does the first hour of NQ trading contain directional information? The answer, after seven phases of systematic testing, is nuanced — and more interesting than a simple yes or no.

ORB momentum alone doesn't work. Mean reversion alone doesn't work. But a 3-state Hidden Markov Model that routes between them based on daily session characteristics produces a system with Sharpe +1.298 out-of-sample, a train-to-test gap of just 0.07 PF, and 87.2% probability of passing an FTMO $100k challenge per Monte Carlo simulation.

The core finding isn't the strategy itself. It's that regime detection — implemented as a GaussianHMM trained on four causal daily features — can meaningfully separate sessions where breakouts follow through from sessions where price reverts to equilibrium. 2026, which destroyed both individual strategies in isolation, produced a Sharpe of +2.366 in the combined system because the HMM correctly routed it to ORB SHORT.

Original hypothesis

"The range formed during the first hour of US market (09:30–10:30 NY) contains directional information. When price breaks that range with momentum and volume above normal, continuation of the move exists."

This hypothesis was rigorously tested and falsified in its original form — t-test p=0.860 on the OOS set. But the process of falsifying it revealed where the real structure was.

Phases 1–2 · Rejected

ORB Momentum Baseline

Classic Opening Range Breakout — tested first on 15-minute bars, then rebuilt on 1-minute bars with identical logic. If price closes above OR High with a strong candle and elevated volume, go long. Symmetric for shorts.

Strategy definition

The Opening Range is the maximum and minimum of all 1-minute bars between 09:30 and 10:30 NY (exclusive). After 10:30, each new bar is evaluated against the breakout conditions.

Long entry
01Bar closes above OR High after 10:30 NY
02Bar range (H−L) > 1.0 × ATR(20) — momentum confirmation
03Bar volume > 1.5 × avg_volume(20) — institutional participation
→Entry at close · SL 1.0×ATR below · TP 2.0×ATR above · max 120 bars
Short entry (symmetric)
01Bar closes below OR Low after 10:30 NY
02Bar range > 1.0 × ATR(20)
03Bar volume > 1.5 × avg_volume(20)
→SL 1.0×ATR above entry · TP 2.0×ATR below · EOD exit 15:45 NY

Why it failed

The diagnostic is unambiguous: 52% of exits were EOD. Price broke the OR, moved partially in the expected direction, and stalled — never reaching the 2×ATR take profit. Only 8–9% of exits were TP hits. The continuation hypothesis is partially true but at a much smaller magnitude than assumed.

The statistical test confirmed this. T-test on OOS returns: p=0.860. We cannot reject the null hypothesis that the mean return is zero. Bootstrap Profit Factor had a 5th percentile of 0.702 — in the worst 5% of scenarios, you'd lose 30% of your capital.

Verdict

ORB momentum has no statistically significant edge on NQ 1-min. The 2×ATR target is geometrically too ambitious for a 2-hour window. The hypothesis of continuation exists but the magnitude is insufficient for the risk parameters as specified.

Phase 3 · No confirmed edge

SMA(50) is vacuously true

Close < OR Low mathematically implies close < SMA(50 bars within session). The breakout condition already guarantees the filter passes. Useful period would require >120 bars spanning multiple sessions.

Trailing at 0.5× ATR kills winners

Win rate rises to 47% but avg winner collapses from $506 to $91. The trailing catches noise before the move develops. R/R implodes to ~1:1 — unprofitable with that win rate.

SHORT shows real but conditional edge

PF 1.12 in test, Sharpe +0.78. Makes macro sense — 2025 was bearish/volatile. Fails in 2023 bull run. A static SHORT-only filter requires regime detection, not static rules.

Best generalization: prev-session filter

Train→test delta of only −0.04 vs −0.26 baseline. Still can't clear PF 1.0 in test. Halves trade count to 192. Correct direction, insufficient magnitude alone.

The regime problem: Every filter that improves PF does so by selecting for a specific market regime. When the regime changes — as it did in H2 2023 — the filter selects exactly the wrong sessions. The solution isn't a better static filter. It's a dynamic regime classifier.

Phase 4B · Rejected

Mean Reversion to OR POC

A structurally opposite hypothesis: trade the rejection, not the breakout. When price touches an OR extreme but fails to close beyond it, enter targeting the OR midpoint — the level of maximum volume concentration during the opening range.

Strategy logic

Long setup · rejection at OR Low
01During OR (09:30–10:29): a bar's Low touches OR Low but closes above it
02No subsequent OR bar closes below OR Low — rejection is not violated
03After 10:30: enter when price rises to 40% of OR range from low
TPOR Midpoint — (or_high + or_low) / 2
SL1.0 × ATR below entry · max 60 bars · no entries after 14:00

Pre-P&L diagnostic

96% of rejection bars had 5+ ticks of penetration. These aren't pin-bar rejections — they're deep extensions that reverse. The "touch and reject" is a failed breakout snapping back. Structurally noisier than classical mean-reversion setups, which explains the structural win rate problem.

The math was close but not there: with median R/R of 2.31:1, the breakeven win rate is 28.6%. Actual achieved: 27.8%. The best variant — 50% OR entry with far extreme TP — reached PF test 0.926 and R/R 4.57:1, but still couldn't hold in 2026.

Verdict

Mean reversion to OR POC is not supported. NQ OR extremes are not clean rejection levels. 96% of touches penetrate 5+ ticks, making reversions unreliable at the win rate the R/R demands. The 2026 trending regime accelerates OOS losses.

Phase 5 · First confirmed OOS edge

HMM Regime Router

Two structurally opposite strategies. Two individually failing systems. They fail in different regimes. A 3-state Hidden Markov Model trained on four causal daily features learns to route each strategy to its favorable conditions.

Features (all causal — available before 10:30 NY)

FeatureDefinitionCaptures
realized_vol std(log returns) of 1-min bars during OR First-hour volatility
or_range / ATR OR amplitude ÷ prior day ATR(20) Relative range size
overnight_gap / ATR (09:30 open − 15:45 close prev day) ÷ ATR Overnight pressure
trend_strength Linear slope of last 5 session closes ÷ ATR Recent directional bias

All features are normalized with a 20-day rolling z-score (strictly causal). HMM re-trained from scratch on each walk-forward window's training set.

The three states

Ranging
38%
→ Mean Reversion LONG
Positive gap, stable vol, high OR. Well-defined range with clear commitment — price tends to revert from extremes.
Trending
54%
→ ORB SHORT
Narrow OR, no gap, slight negative slope. Intraday directional pressure — breakouts tend to extend.
Volatile
8%
→ No trade
OR >36× ATR, large negative gaps. Both strategies fail. Not trading is itself a profitable decision here.

Why 2026 became the best year

2026 was dominated by trending sessions (61.9%). The HMM routed these to ORB SHORT, which has standalone PF 1.37 in 2026 and PF 1.95 within the combined system. Before the HMM, long trades in 2026 were destroying the result. The HMM simply stopped activating them — no prediction needed, just routing.

First OOS edge confirmed

PF test 1.104 with a train→test gap of only 0.07 PF — lowest in the entire project. Sharpe test +0.04 (first positive). 2026 Sharpe +2.366. The HMM doesn't predict direction — it identifies which playbook fits today's session character.

Key findings

Optimal sizing confirmed

0.25% risk per trade = 1 contract on $100k. P(success) 87.2%. Median DD 1.5% during successful paths. p95 DD 6.4% — inside FTMO's 10% hard limit.

Time horizon is the main limitation

$35.75 expectancy × ~10 trades/month → ~280 trades → ~26 months to reach $10k. This is a function of R/R=1:1, not win rate. The fix is wider TP, not higher frequency.

Stress test reveals fragility

Win rate −20% (54.6% → 43.8%) collapses P(pass) from 87.2% to 2.0%. With R/R of 1:1, the system lives and dies by win rate maintenance. Zero margin for degradation.

Next research priority

R/R to ≥1.5:1 would raise expectancy per trade and make the system resilient to win rate degradation. The structural fix is on the take profit, not the frequency.

Limitations and next directions

The R/R fragility: The optimized system has win rate 54.6% but R/R of 1.03:1. A 20% degradation in win rate collapses P(FTMO pass) from 87% to 2%. The priority for the next research cycle is achieving R/R ≥1.5:1 without destroying win rate — or understanding why price fails to reach wider targets.

The infrastructure built here — roll-schedule CSV loader, causal indicator engine, event-driven backtester with High/Low SL/TP simulation, walk-forward framework, and bootstrap Monte Carlo — is fully reusable. The next cycle on a new hypothesis (VWAP reversion, gap fill, failed auction) starts from a much stronger foundation.

Final verdict

This is a complete, honest quantitative research cycle. The ORB momentum hypothesis was tested, falsified, and refined into a regime-aware system with confirmed OOS edge — Sharpe +1.298, PF 1.223, 87.2% FTMO pass probability. It is not a finished product. The R/R needs work and the win rate is fragile. But the methodology is sound, the infrastructure is solid, and the HMM routing insight is real.
